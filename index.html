Our Story

Our story started when we saw the polluted school lake's water quality improve. That made us think of a video we'd seen: a blogger cleaning garbage in a remote Guilin ditch. Unlike well-attended waters like our school lake, many far-flung waterways are overlooked because of their location‚Äîso we launched this project.

At first, we considered using small underwater robots for monitoring. But they were too costly, easily stuck by aquatic plants and mud, and unable to access narrow river channels‚Äîmaking this approach unfeasible. Then we noticed plastic bottles floating on the water, and it hit us: floating objects can reach wherever the current flows. The water itself would act as natural navigation for our device.

Design Principle

We designed the shape of our product in the shape of a bowl to be stable and capable of floating according to Newtonian principles, since the working environment of the object is in water.

According to Newtonian physics, the magnitude of buoyancy force is the product of the density of water, the gravitational constant, and the volume of water the object discharges when it enters the water, which is the same as the volume of the object submerged in water. When the object experiences maximum buoyancy force, the volume of the discharged water is the same as the object's volume.

The bowl is designed to contain both material and empty space. The existence of empty space makes the bowl's volume larger and that makes the bowl capable of experiencing greater buoyancy. Therefore, the bowl is less likely to sink when facing great pressure.

Making the shape of our product two bowls is the best way to increase the maximum buoyancy force that can be pushed out of this design to ensure least risk to sink.

Our Process

1

Mold modeling

We used Autodesk fusion software to build the spherical shape. Our model consists the upper and lower bowl and the middle baffle.

2

Sensor testing

We collected water sample from our school and set up the three sensors to test them. We connected them to the main board, and the main board is connected to the computer. We tested if the measured values and the overall analysis can be presented normally on the display end.

3

Building up

We placed the sensors and the ceramic power generating board in place and made the oscillating block that hits the power generating board to provide energy. We connected the circuit board of the sensors with the battery for energy supply and the main board for information delivery. We also connected the battery with other forms of energy generator using various wires. Then, we plucked holes on the baffle and the edge of the bowls and connected them with nails and screws.

4

Future Plan

We decide to practice the function of measuring and analyzing data of our machine by putting it into practical use. We decide to use it to determine the water quality of the Kunming lake in the Summer Palace beside our school.
